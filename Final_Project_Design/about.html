Purpose

Personal chefs frequently operate alone or in small groups in private kitchens. They need to be capable of preparing all kinds of food and creating highly personalized menus for their customers. A personal chef can prepare and ship meals to be reheated upon delivery, or they can cook on location for a client. Being a chef is my comfort zone because it allows me to express love and emotion through food.

Cook or Chef

What makes a cook different from a chef? To put it succinctly, a chef is a person who has received training in flavor analysis, culinary arts, recipe creation using fresh ingredients, and high levels of responsibility in the kitchen. A cook is a person who prepares food according to set recipes. A chef, also called a head chef, executive chef, or chef de cuisine, is a qualified cook who manages every area of a kitchen. A chef must possess a wide range of skills, including hiring, firing, and training kitchen staff as well as controlling and managing inventories, purchasing food supplies and cooking equipment, maintaining clean and safe cooking practices, managing kitchen expenses, designing plates, and creating menus. A chef's skill in guiding you on a culinary adventure with credentials from learning with food is what makes them superior to a cook.
